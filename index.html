Kiosk Design

This project involved conceptualisation of a self billing kiosk which can be used for quick check-out at megastores such as BigBazaar. The entire process involved inspection of user behaviour during the check out and the buying-payment patterns. The observations took into account the ergonomics as well as the different shelves, temporary storage spaces, and promotional areas within the kiosk structure itself. Multiple iterations were made before realising the product in virtual space. The concept design was further taken forward for implementation.

Teewe Campaign Design

Campaign design for tech startup Teewe for their new product launch included ensuring the visibility of the brand and its upcoming product to a wider audience. This involved strategising the promotional campaigns over social media, mailers, hoardings and informational posters. This also included designing mascot and strengthening the brand position by working in collaboration with the design and marketing team.

Truck Art Prints

This project involved creation of prints for home-decor and lifestyle merchandising store Mother Earth. These prints were inspired from the artworks done to decorate the trucks which incidentally originated from present day Pakistan and can also be seen throughout India. These hand painted piece of designs have a flavour of their own. Similar approach was taken while designing these prints. The final outputs were digital prints drawing inspirations from hand craft.

Street Food Branding

This project involved branding of food item which is native to its place. Litti-Chokha is an indigenous dish of Bihar. For this, brand identity was created along with a catchy tag line which would appeal to a vast majority of classes with respect to both language, and visual communication. Along with the brand identity, the packaging and other items for promotions were created as well.

Textile Work Showcase

Textile prints were created over the period of the course at NIFT, as well as for textile mills and merchandising stores which involved development of multiple iterations of designs through various techniques such as batik, tie and die, block print, screen print etc. These prints were outcomes of both hand made art and digital media.
